Theory

Music theory can seem boring but it's the solid foundation every musician needs. The good news is that you don’t need to learn how to read sheet music or understand complicated theory to start. In this section, we’ll mainly use a piano layout to explain the theory because it's one of the easiest and most visual ways to understand how music works.

Basic

Before diving into a lot of music theory, it's helpful to understand one simple fact: music is built on just 12 different notes. You’ve probably heard of the classic ‘Do Re Mi Fa Sol La Si Do’ these are the basic building blocks of melodies. In modern music, we often use letters to represent them:

  • Do = C
  • Re = D
  • Mi = E
  • Fa = F
  • Sol = G
  • La = A
  • Si = B

key layout

Numerous keys are available for diatonic harmonicas, which are the 10-hole instruments utilized many music. Harmonicas are typically key-specific, prompting musicians to collect multiple instruments to cover a range of musical keys. The standard tuning system for diatonic harmonicas is referred to as Richter tuning. The key of C is the most often used harmonica key. Position

earning about positions on the harmonica can be a bit confusing at first, but it's really useful. It simply means the key you're playing in, depending on where you start and which notes you focus on. Understanding positions helps you play in different styles like blues or folk using the same harmonica.
