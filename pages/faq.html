What harmonica should I start with?

We recommend starting with a 10-hole diatonic harmonica in the key of C, as most beginner lessons and tabs are based on this model.

What’s the difference between diatonic and chromatic?

Diatonic harmonicas are simple and limited to one key, while chromatic harmonicas have a button to access all notes and are used in more complex music.

Do I need to learn music theory to play harmonica?

No, you can begin with tabs and simple songs, and learn theory gradually as you progress.

What are harmonica tabs and how do I read them?

Harmonica tabs use numbers to show which hole to play and a minus sign to indicate a draw note.

How do I clean my harmonica?

Tap it gently to remove moisture after playing, and occasionally clean the parts with warm water and a soft brush if the harmonica allows disassembly.

How do I know if I’m bending notes correctly?

If your pitch drops smoothly and matches the target note on a tuner or bending app, you’re doing it right.

Can I play harmonica with any kind of music?

Yes, harmonica can be played in blues, folk, country, rock, pop, and even jazz styles.

How often should I practice?

Practicing just 10 to 15 minutes a day is enough to make steady progress.
